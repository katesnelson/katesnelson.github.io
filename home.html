Today's world of fast-paced social and environmental change can often seem chaotic and out-of-control, however I firmly believe that it is possible to steer that change in more positive directions that provide long-term benefits for both society and the environment. My research attempts to contribute to the body of knowledge on sustainable planning and policy development for socioenvironmental systems by evaluating the impacts of existing regulations and programs designed to manage the distribution of natural resources and impacts of natural hazards and by investigating the underlying conditions and processes of systems that lead to improved sustainability outcomes.

Research

Investigating the drivers of resilience, vulnerability, and adaptive capacity and their impacts on social-environmental systems using spatiotemporally explicit modeling and behavior driver simulation methodologies within an interdisciplinary framing.

Teaching

Preparing students for roles as future scientists, engineers, and decision-makers through interdisciplinary, problem-based and investigative learning.
