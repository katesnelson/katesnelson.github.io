About Me

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at Vanderbilt University where I study Environmental Management and Policy. My studies have emphasized consideration of multiple disciplinary perspectives (engineering, environmental science, management, law, and sociology) and adoption of methodolgical techniques from these and other disciplines (such as policy analysis and epidemiology). My research focuses on analysis of social structures that interact with the built and natural environment to promote or harm social equity, ecological health, and long-term viability of social-environmental systems with a methodological emphasis on consideration of spatial and temporal dependencies. I will be on the job market in Fall 2017.

Research

Investigating the drivers of resilience, vulnerability, and adaptive capacity and their impacts on social-environmental systems using spatiotemporally explicit modeling and behavior driver simulation methodologies within an interdisciplinary framing.

Teaching

My goal as an educator is to introduce students to complex problems facing our worls and diverse perspectives on problem solving.
